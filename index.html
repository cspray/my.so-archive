My Stack Overflow Archive

Stack Overflow is one of my favorite, most-visited sites on the Internet. I really love the concept and, more importantly, the execution of the concept. The Stack Exchange sites were a big part of me getting a job in the development industry and I think as a solo developer looking to become professional getting involved in a community is pretty important. Basically, Stack Overflow is great.

Sometimes though, they do things I don't agree with and take down valuable, but unfit, content. I understand the rationale, keeping the site pruned of wasteful material is a great thing that makes Stack Overflow better and more effective than its competitors. I also understand though that some of the content being removed is winding up being some of the very same content that was instrumental in me, ya know, *becoming a developer.* This is my attempt to gather up that material or anything else I find on the chopping block that might be useful to keep around.
